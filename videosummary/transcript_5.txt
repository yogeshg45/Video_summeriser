For the matrix

$$
M(x)=\begin{pmatrix}
\cos x & \sin x & \sin x\\
\sin x & \cos x & \sin x\\
\sin x & \sin x & \cos x
\end{pmatrix},
$$

one shows by direct factorization that

$$
\det M(x) \;=\; (\cos x + 2\sin x)\,(\cos x - \sin x)^2.
$$

On $[-\,\tfrac{\pi}{4},\,\tfrac{\pi}{4}]$, this vanishes precisely when either

1. **$\cos x - \sin x=0$**, i.e.\ $\tan x=1$, giving $x=\tfrac\pi4$;
2. **$\cos x +2\sin x=0$**, i.e.\ $\tan x=-\tfrac12$, giving

   $$
   x=\arctan\bigl(-\tfrac12\bigr)\approx -0.4636\,.
   $$

Both of these lie in the closed interval, and no other solutions occur there.
**Hence there are exactly two distinct real roots.**
